1. Basic Sentence Structure
Chinese follows Subject + Verb + Object (SVO) — same as English.

我 喝 水。(Wǒ hē shuǐ.) — I drink water.
她 吃 苹果。(Tā chī píngguǒ.) — She eats an apple.
2. The verb 是 (shì) — "to be"
Used to link nouns. Not used with adjectives.

我是学生。(Wǒ shì xuésheng.) — I am a student.
他是老师。(Tā shì lǎoshī.) — He is a teacher.

Negative: 不是 (bú shì)
我不是医生。— I am not a doctor.
3. Questions with 吗 (ma)
Add 吗 to the end of any statement to make a yes/no question.

你好吗？(Nǐ hǎo ma?) — Are you well?
他是老师吗？— Is he a teacher?

Answer with the verb: 是 (yes) / 不是 (no)
4. Negation with 不 (bù/bú)
Place 不 before the verb to negate it.

我不喝茶。— I don't drink tea.
她不去。— She is not going.

Exception: 有 (to have) negates with 没: 我没有。
5. Numbers & Measure Words
You can't say "three book" — you need a measure word between the number and noun.

个 (gè) — general: 三个人 (3 people)
本 (běn) — books: 两本书 (2 books)
杯 (bēi) — cups: 一杯水 (1 cup of water)
只 (zhī) — animals: 一只猫 (1 cat)
6. Pronouns
我 wǒ — I/me  |  你 nǐ — you  |  他/她/它 tā — he/she/it
我们 wǒmen — we  |  你们 nǐmen — you (plural)  |  他们 tāmen — they

Add 的 after pronouns for possession:
我的书 — my book  |  她的猫 — her cat
7. Time words come before the verb
Unlike English, time expressions go before the verb (but after the subject).

我今天去学校。— I today go to school. (I'm going to school today.)
她明天不来。— She tomorrow not come. (She's not coming tomorrow.)
8. 有 (yǒu) — "to have" / "there is"
我有一个弟弟。— I have a younger brother.
这里有水。— There is water here.

Negative: 没有 (méiyǒu) — don't have / there isn't
我没有钱。— I have no money.
9. 的 (de) — Possession & Description
Links a descriptor to a noun.

我的 — my  |  大的狗 — big dog
很好的老师 — a very good teacher
10. 很 (hěn) — "very" with adjectives
Adjectives in predicates usually take 很, even when not meaning "very".

她很漂亮。— She is (very) pretty.
天气很好。— The weather is (very) good.

Without 很 it implies contrast: 她漂亮，他不漂亮。

1. Completed Actions with 了 (le)
Place 了 after the verb to indicate a completed action.

我吃了饭。(Wǒ chī le fàn.) — I have eaten.
她买了一本书。(Tā mǎi le yī běn shū.) — She bought a book.

Negative: Use 没 (méi) instead — drop 了.
我没吃饭。— I haven't eaten.
2. Actions in Progress with 在 (zài) / 正在
Place 在 or 正在 before the verb to indicate ongoing action (like -ing).

他在看电视。(Tā zài kàn diànshì.) — He is watching TV.
我正在学习。(Wǒ zhèngzài xuéxí.) — I am studying right now.

Add 呢 at the end to emphasise continuity: 她睡觉呢。— She's sleeping.
3. Comparative Structure: A 比 B + Adj
Use 比 (bǐ) to compare two things.

他比我高。(Tā bǐ wǒ gāo.) — He is taller than me.
今天比昨天冷。— Today is colder than yesterday.

Negative: 没有 (méiyǒu) — A 没有 B + Adj
我没有他高。— I am not as tall as him.
4. Experience with 过 (guò)
Place 过 after the verb to express a past experience.

我去过北京。(Wǒ qùguo Běijīng.) — I have been to Beijing (before).
你吃过北京烤鸭吗？— Have you ever eaten Peking duck?

Negative: 没 + verb + 过
我没去过上海。— I have never been to Shanghai.
5. Duration of Time
Place time duration after the verb to say how long something lasted.

我学了两年中文。— I studied Chinese for two years.
他睡了八个小时。— He slept for eight hours.

For ongoing actions: 我学中文学了两年了。— I've been studying Chinese for two years (and still am).
6. Modal Verbs: 能, 可以, 会, 要, 应该
能 (néng) — can (physical ability/permission): 我能游泳。— I can swim.
可以 (kěyǐ) — may/allowed to: 你可以进来。— You may come in.
会 (huì) — can (learned skill): 她会说法语。— She can speak French.
要 (yào) — want to / need to / going to: 我要去。— I want/am going to go.
应该 (yīnggāi) — should: 你应该休息。— You should rest.
7. Pivotal Sentences with 让/叫/请
These verbs take a person as their object who then does something.

妈妈让我去买菜。— Mum told me to go buy vegetables.
老师叫我们读书。— The teacher told us to read.
请你帮我一下。— Please help me.
8. Result Complements
Attach a result to the verb to show the outcome.

写完 (xiě wán) — finish writing: 我写完了。— I've finished writing.
听懂 (tīng dǒng) — hear and understand: 你听懂了吗？— Did you understand?
吃饱 (chī bǎo) — eat until full: 我吃饱了。— I'm full.
做好 (zuò hǎo) — do well/finish: 作业做好了。— The homework is done.
9. Topic-Comment Structure
Chinese often puts the topic first, then comments on it.

这个问题，我不知道。— This question, I don't know (the answer).
那个地方，我没去过。— That place, I've never been.

This is very common and gives sentences a natural flow.
10. 一…就… (yī…jiù…) — As soon as
Expresses that one action immediately follows another.

我一到家就吃饭。— As soon as I get home I eat.
他一说话，我就笑了。— As soon as he spoke, I laughed.
11. 先…然后… — First… then…
先 (xiān) means "first" and 然后 (ránhòu) means "then/after that".

我先吃饭，然后去学校。— I'll eat first, then go to school.
先洗手，然后吃东西。— First wash your hands, then eat.
12. Expressing "Both… and…" with 又…又…
又…又… connects two adjectives or actions.

这个菜又便宜又好吃。— This dish is both cheap and delicious.
她又聪明又漂亮。— She is both smart and pretty.
